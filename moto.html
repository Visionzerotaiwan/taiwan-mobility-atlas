01
規模與趨勢：十年沒有變好Scale & Trend — A Decade That Didn't Improve
Scale & Trend

機車是台灣道路死亡的主體，而且這個事實十年來沒有鬆動。2016 年全國機車騎士 A1 死亡 984 人、佔當年所有道路死亡 61.3%；到 2025 年，絕對數字升到 1,054 人，佔比更攀到 64.2%。中間 2019–2022 年甚至連年站上 1,150 人以上的高原，2022 年以 1,212 人寫下十年新高。Motorcyclists are the backbone of Taiwan's road deaths, and that has not loosened in ten years. In 2016, 984 motorcyclists died (A1, within 24 h) — 61.3% of all road deaths that year. By 2025 the absolute number had risen to 1,054, and the share climbed to 64.2%. From 2019 to 2022 it sat on a plateau above 1,150 a year, peaking at 1,212 in 2022 — a decade high.

換句話說，過去十年無論「行人地獄」如何躍上國際版面、無論政策口號如何更迭，死在路上的人，主力始終是機車騎士，而且占比不降反升。這不是因為機車變得更危險，而是因為其他運具（特別是汽車駕駛與乘客）的防護隨著車體安全科技持續改善，唯獨暴露在外、缺乏潰縮空間的兩輪族群，被獨自留在風險的最前線。In other words, however often "pedestrian hell" made international headlines, however the policy slogans changed, the people dying on the road have always been mainly motorcyclists — and the share is rising, not falling. Not because motorcycles got more dangerous, but because every other mode (above all car drivers and passengers) keeps getting safer as vehicle-body technology improves, leaving the exposed two-wheeled population — with no crumple zone — alone at the front line of risk.

02
誰在死亡：年輕人與長者的雙峰Who Dies — A Bimodal Age Curve
Who Dies — A Bimodal Age Curve

把死亡騎士的年齡攤開，會看到一條清楚的「雙峰」曲線：18–24 歲的年輕騎士是單一最大族群（占可辨識死者的 19.8%），另一個高峰落在 65 歲以上的高齡者（合計 26.1%）。中壯年雖然每個年齡層都有死亡，但密度明顯較低。Lay out the ages of the riders who died and a clear "twin-peak" curve appears: riders aged 18–24 are the single largest group (19.8% of identifiable victims), with a second peak among those 65 and older (26.1% combined). The middle-aged die in every bracket too, but at noticeably lower density.

這兩個高峰背後是兩種完全不同的事故腳本。年輕騎士的死亡，較多與速度、夜間、酒精與經驗不足有關——剛拿到駕照、通勤與夜生活的移動需求高，風險辨識卻尚未成熟。高齡騎士則相反：他們多在日間、低速、住家附近的路口因路權衝突而身故，問題不在魯莽，而在反應時間、視野與身體脆弱度——同樣一場 40 公里的側撞，年輕人可能重傷、長者卻可能致命。The two peaks hide two completely different crash scripts. Young riders' deaths skew toward speed, night, alcohol and inexperience — newly licensed, high mobility demand for commuting and nightlife, but risk perception not yet mature. Elderly riders are the opposite: they mostly die in daylight, at low speed, at junctions near home through right-of-way conflicts. The problem isn't recklessness but reaction time, field of view and physical fragility — the same 40 km/h side impact may injure a young rider but kill an older one.

性別上，男性占 72.8%、女性占 27.2%，與整體 A1 死者的性別比一致。值得注意的是，高齡層的女性比例明顯上升，反映長輩女性在社區短程移動中對機車的高度依賴。By sex, men are 72.8% and women 27.2%, matching the overall A1 death ratio. Notably, the female share rises sharply among the elderly — reflecting how heavily older women rely on the scooter for short trips around the neighbourhood.

Young riders need driver training and speed management; elderly riders need junction design and alternative transport. Campaigns that lump the two peaks together usually miss both.
03
為什麼會死：路權衝突與分心是主旋律Why They Die — Right-of-Way & Inattention
Primary Causes

將 7,739 筆有詳細肇因紀錄的案件歸類後，最大宗的死因不是「飆車」，而是日常的路權與注意力失誤：「未依規定讓車（路權衝突）」占 20.4%、「未注意車前狀態／分心」占 19.4%，兩者合計近四成。接著是「違反號誌、標誌」13.1% 與「違規轉彎、變換車道」10.5%。Classifying the 7,739 cases with detailed cause records, the leading killer is not "racing" but everyday right-of-way and attention failures: "failure to yield (right-of-way conflict)" 20.4% and "inattention / distraction" 19.4% — together nearly four in ten. Then "signal / sign violations" 13.1% and "improper turning / lane changes" 10.5%.

這組數字顛覆了「機車死亡＝騎士違規亂竄」的刻板印象。路權衝突的本質，是兩方對誰該先行的認知落差——而在汽機車混流、號誌與標線設計不友善的台灣道路上，騎士往往是衝突中肉身最沒有保護的一方。換言之，許多死亡並非單純「誰對誰錯」，而是道路環境本身把碰撞的致命後果，系統性地分配給了騎士。These numbers overturn the stereotype that "motorcyclist deaths = riders weaving recklessly." A right-of-way conflict is, at root, two parties disagreeing about who goes first — and on Taiwan's roads, where cars and scooters mix and signals and markings are unfriendly, the rider is usually the most unprotected body in the conflict. Many deaths are not simply about "who was right"; rather, the road environment itself systematically allocates the fatal consequences of a collision to the rider.

值得單獨點名的是酒（毒）駕仍占 6.9%（532 人），逆向行駛 2.0%、超速／未減速 4.5%。這些「高致命、可預防」的行為加總超過一成，是執法與工程能立即著力的部分。另有約 14% 列為「不明」或「其他」，反映基層事故調查在因果判定上的侷限。Worth naming separately: drink/drug driving is still 6.9% (532 people), wrong-way driving 2.0%, speeding/failure-to-slow 4.5%. These "highly lethal, highly preventable" behaviours add up to over a tenth — the part enforcement and engineering can act on immediately. About 14% are logged as "unknown" or "other," reflecting the limits of frontline crash investigation in assigning cause.

更深一層的真相藏在「肇事責任」與「死亡」的錯位裡。在多數機車對汽車的致命碰撞中，警方紀錄的第一當事人（肇事方）往往是汽車駕駛，而失去生命的卻是機車騎士——肇責與後果，由不同的人承擔。這正是脆弱用路人問題的核心：同樣一個失誤，發生在鋼鐵車殼裡只是擦撞，發生在血肉之軀上卻是致命。因此「降低機車肇事率」與「降低機車死亡率」是兩件事——前者靠教育騎士，後者更靠改造道路與約束大型車輛，讓任何人的一次失誤都不必用一條人命來償付。A deeper truth hides in the gap between "fault" and "death." In most fatal motorcycle-versus-car crashes, the first party (the at-fault party) recorded by police is often the car driver, while the one who dies is the motorcyclist — fault and consequence borne by different people. This is the heart of the vulnerable-road-user problem: the same mistake is a fender-bender inside a steel shell, but fatal to a body of flesh. So "cutting the motorcycle crash rate" and "cutting the motorcycle death rate" are two different things — the first leans on educating riders, the second on redesigning roads and restraining heavy vehicles, so that no one's single mistake has to be paid for with a life.

04
怎麼撞：七成是「被別人撞」How They Crash — 7 in 10 Are Hit by Another
Crash Configuration

機車死亡事故中，高達 72% 涉及另一輛車（多為汽車或大型車），僅 28% 是騎士自摔或自撞固定物的單車事故。這說明絕大多數的騎士之死，發生在「與他人互動」的瞬間——被左轉車切到、被右方來車側撞、在路口被未讓車的對向車輛攔腰撞上。In fatal motorcycle crashes, a striking 72% involve another vehicle (mostly cars or heavy vehicles); only 28% are single-vehicle crashes where the rider falls or hits a fixed object. So the vast majority of rider deaths happen in a moment of "interaction with others" — cut off by a left-turning car, side-struck by a vehicle from the right, T-boned at a junction by an oncoming vehicle that failed to yield.

從碰撞細類看，側撞、路口交岔撞、追撞是三大型態。側撞與路口撞共同指向同一個結構問題：路口的衝突點管理失靈。而單車事故中的「路上翻車摔倒」「撞護欄、路樹、電桿」則暴露了郊區道路的硬體致命性——一旦失控衝出路面，路側幾乎沒有任何寬容設計來吸收撞擊。By collision sub-type, side impacts, junction collisions and rear-enders are the big three. Side and junction impacts point to one structural problem: failed conflict-point management at junctions. The single-vehicle "overturn / fall" and "hit guardrail, tree, pole" expose the lethal hardware of rural roads — once a rider loses control and leaves the carriageway, the roadside has almost no forgiving design to absorb the impact.

反直覺：Counter-intuitive:「機車死亡多是騎士自己摔死」是錯的——七成是與其他車輛的碰撞。降低騎士死亡，關鍵不只在「管騎士」，更在「讓汽車駕駛看得到、讓得了機車」。"Most motorcyclists just fall and kill themselves" is wrong — seven in ten are collisions with another vehicle. Cutting rider deaths is not only about "managing riders" but about "letting car drivers see, and yield to, motorcycles."
05
天候與光線：殺人的是好天氣Weather & Light — The Fair-Weather Paradox
Weather & Light — The Fair-Weather Paradox

最反直覺的一張圖：81.8% 的機車死亡發生在晴天，雨天僅占 7.4%；路面狀態同樣有九成是「乾燥」。這不代表雨天安全，而是晴天的騎乘量遠高於雨天、且晴天車速更快——當能見度好、路面乾、心理戒備低時，速度與流量才是真正的致命變數。把資源全押在「雨天小心」的宣導，等於對著最小的風險開槍。The most counter-intuitive chart of all: 81.8% of motorcyclist deaths happen in clear weather, with rain only 7.4%; nine in ten road surfaces are "dry." This doesn't mean rain is safe — it means far more riding happens on clear days, and speeds are higher then. When visibility is good, the road is dry and mental guard is down, speed and volume are the real lethal variables. Pouring resources into "take care in the rain" campaigns is firing at the smallest risk.

光線則揭露另一個可工程化的破口。日間事故占 43.4%，但夜間（含晨暮）合計達 56.6%；其中近半數（占全部的 24.6%）發生在「照明不足或無照明」的路段。對缺乏車體燈光優勢、體積又小的機車而言，夜間照明不只是舒適問題，而是「能不能被看見」的生死問題。路燈，是被低估的救命基礎建設。Light exposes another engineerable gap. Daytime crashes are 43.4%, but night (including dawn/dusk) totals 56.6%; nearly half of those (24.6% of all) happen on stretches that are "poorly lit or unlit." For a small machine with no big headlamp advantage, night lighting isn't a comfort issue — it is the life-or-death question of "can you be seen." Street lighting is an underrated piece of life-saving infrastructure.

06
好發路段：50 公里的市區路口Where — The 50 km/h Urban Junction
Where — The 50 km/h Urban Junction

把「在哪裡死」拆開，輪廓非常清晰：57.8% 在市區道路、54.6% 在速限 50 公里的路段、48% 發生在路口。最典型的致命場景，是一條速限 50、汽機車混流、號誌與轉向動線複雜的市區幹道路口。Break down "where they die" and the profile is sharp: 57.8% on urban roads, 54.6% on 50 km/h stretches, 48% at junctions. The most typical fatal scene is a junction on an urban arterial — 50 km/h limit, cars and scooters mixed, signals and turning paths complex.

路口為何如此致命？因為它是所有衝突點的交會處——左轉、右轉、直行、行人穿越在同一塊空間裡爭道，而台灣的路口普遍缺乏專用轉向時相、機車庇護空間與清晰的視距。速限 50 看似不快，但對血肉之軀的騎士，50 公里的撞擊已遠超人體耐受極限。Why are junctions so lethal? Because they are where every conflict point meets — left turns, right turns, through traffic and pedestrian crossings all competing in one space, while Taiwan's junctions generally lack dedicated turn phases, motorcycle refuge space and clear sightlines. A 50 km/h limit seems modest, but for a rider of flesh and blood, a 50 km/h impact is already far beyond human tolerance.

另一條被忽略的戰線是鄉村道路：省道、縣道、鄉道與村里道路合計占了約 39% 的死亡。這些路段速限高、照明差、救護遙遠，一旦失控往往直接致命。鄉村的致命性，很大一部分來自「救援空白」——車禍後能否在「黃金一小時」內送醫，城鄉差距巨大。同一場撞擊，在台北可能 10 分鐘內進急診，在偏鄉卻可能因路途遙遠、救護資源稀薄而錯過急救時機。都會的問題在「路口衝突密度」，鄉村的問題在「速度、照明與救援時間」，兩者需要截然不同的對策：前者要靠號誌時相、庇護空間與速度管理，後者要靠路側淨空、照明補強與緊急醫療網的城鄉再平衡。A second, overlooked front is the rural road: provincial, county, township and village roads together account for about 39% of deaths. These stretches have high speed limits, poor lighting and distant emergency care — once control is lost, the outcome is often immediately fatal. Much of rural lethality comes from the "rescue gap" — whether a crash victim reaches care within the "golden hour" varies enormously between city and country. The same impact might reach an ER within 10 minutes in Taipei, but in a remote area the distance and thin ambulance coverage can miss the window. The metro problem is "junction conflict density"; the rural problem is "speed, lighting and rescue time." They need opposite remedies: the former through signal phasing, refuge space and speed management; the latter through roadside clear zones, lighting upgrades and an urban-rural rebalancing of the emergency medical network.

什麼時候：兩個通勤尖峰When — Two Commuting Peaks
When — Two Commuting Peaks

死亡時間分布呈現典型的「通勤雙峰」：早上 7–9 時與下午 4–6 時是兩個明顯高點，正是上班上學與下班放學的車流尖峰。深夜 2–4 時雖然車少，死亡密度（每單位車流的致死率）卻最高，與酒駕、疲勞、超速高度重疊。The time-of-day distribution shows a classic "commuting twin-peak": 7–9 am and 4–6 pm are the two clear highs — exactly the rush hours for work and school. Late at night, 2–4 am, traffic is light yet death density (lethality per unit of traffic) is highest, overlapping heavily with drink-driving, fatigue and speeding.

這條曲線提醒我們：機車死亡主要是「日常移動」的代價，而非少數極端時刻的意外。當一個社會把絕大多數通勤者推上機車，尖峰時段的死亡就成了系統的結構性產物——解法不在勸導個別騎士「小心一點」，而在提供安全的替代運具與保護性的尖峰車流管理。The curve reminds us that motorcyclist death is mainly the price of "everyday mobility," not a freak event at rare moments. When a society pushes the great majority of commuters onto scooters, rush-hour deaths become a structural output of the system — and the fix is not to nag individual riders to "be careful," but to provide safe alternative transport and protective peak-hour traffic management.

縣市差異：機車死亡是南方與鄉村的故事Geography of Risk — A Southern, Rural Story
Geography of Risk

機車死亡佔比的城鄉落差極大。屏東（71.5%）、金門（70.8%）、高雄（70.2%）、台南（67.7%）等南部與離島縣市，每 10 名道路死者就有 7 人是騎士；相對地，台北市僅 47.7%、基隆 51.2%、台東 54.7% 明顯較低。The urban-rural gap in the motorcyclist share is enormous. In southern and offshore counties — Pingtung (71.5%), Kinmen (70.8%), Kaohsiung (70.2%), Tainan (67.7%) — 7 of every 10 road deaths are riders. By contrast, Taipei City is only 47.7%, Keelung 51.2% and Taitung 54.7% — markedly lower.

這條光譜幾乎完美對應大眾運輸的可及性：台北擁有最密集的捷運與公車網，市民有「不騎車也能移動」的選項，機車死亡佔比自然最低；而南部與鄉村縣市公共運輸稀疏，機車是唯一務實的日常工具，死亡也就高度集中在兩輪。機車安全的終極解方，是讓人們「可以不必騎車」——這正是公共運輸投資的安全紅利。This spectrum maps almost perfectly onto public-transport access: Taipei has the densest metro and bus network, so residents have the option to "move without riding," and the motorcyclist death share is naturally lowest. In southern and rural counties, public transport is sparse, the scooter is the only practical daily tool, and death concentrates on two wheels. The ultimate fix for motorcycle safety is to let people "not have to ride" — which is precisely the safety dividend of investing in public transport.

十年、10,891 條人命，主因不是飆車，而是日常通勤途中、晴天的市區路口、被一輛沒看見你的車側撞。機車安全不是「騎士的個人修養問題」，而是「我們把什麼樣的道路與運輸選擇，留給最脆弱的人」的制度問題。Ten years, 10,891 lives. The main cause is not racing but the ordinary commute — a clear-weather urban junction, side-struck by a car that never saw you. Motorcycle safety is not "a question of riders' personal virtue" but an institutional question: what kind of roads and transport choices do we leave to the most vulnerable? 不只是受害者：機車是系統的產物，也是外部性的來源Not Only Victims — A Systemic Reading
Not Only Victims — A Systemic Reading

前面八節，我們看見騎士是道路系統的受害者。但要誠實，就必須同時說出另一半：機車本身，也是一種把成本外溢給整個社會的運具。台灣機車保有量已逾 1,466 萬輛（2025 年初），密度位居世界前列——它帶來的不只是死傷，還有噪音、空污、碳排，以及對街道與人行空間的長期占據。The first eight sections showed riders as victims of the road system. But honesty requires the other half: the motorcycle is also a mode that spills costs onto society at large. Taiwan's registered scooter fleet exceeds 14.66 million (early 2025), among the highest densities in the world — and it brings not only death and injury but noise, air pollution, carbon, and a long-term occupation of street and pedestrian space.

這裡藏著一個殘酷的對稱：騎士既是污染的製造者之一，也是承受最深的受害者。當你被困在車陣縫隙裡前進，吸進的廢氣濃度遠高於坐在車內的人。死亡、噪音、空污——這些都不是「騎士的道德缺陷」，而是系統的結構性產出。當一個社會把一千四百多萬人推上機車坐墊，這些外部成本就成了系統的必然輸出，而非個別選擇的偶然。Here lies a cruel symmetry: the rider is both one of the polluters and the most exposed victim. Threading through gaps in traffic, you breathe exhaust far more concentrated than someone inside a car. Death, noise, air pollution — none of these is "a rider's moral failing"; they are structural outputs of the system. When a society puts over fourteen million people on a scooter seat, these external costs become an inevitable output of the system, not an accident of individual choice.

所以請記得第二節那條年齡曲線：18–24 歲是單一最大的死亡族群。這不是因為年輕人特別魯莽，而是因為對一個剛離家、買不起汽車、所在城市又沒有捷運可搭的年輕人來說，機車幾乎是唯一務實的選項。他不是「選擇」了風險——是系統替他做了選擇，讓他在毫無意識的情況下，坐上了那張坐墊。把責任推給「個人不小心」，等於要求最沒有議價能力的人，獨自承擔整個系統的設計失敗。So recall that age curve from Section 2: 18–24 is the single largest death group. Not because the young are especially reckless, but because for someone who has just left home, can't afford a car, and lives in a city with no metro to take, the scooter is almost the only practical option. He did not "choose" the risk — the system chose for him, and seated him, unawares, on that saddle. Pinning the blame on "individual carelessness" asks the person with the least bargaining power to carry the entire system's design failure alone.

但這不是要把矛頭轉向騎士。But this is not turning the blame toward riders.恰恰相反：正因為外部性是系統造成的，解方與責任也應該回到系統——回到那些設計、生產、並從現狀中獲利的環節。這是底下兩節的主題。Quite the opposite: precisely because the externalities are created by the system, the solutions and the responsibility should return to the system — to those who design, produce and profit from the status quo. That is the subject of the next two sections.
10
運具移轉：讓人「可以不騎車」的三重紅利Modal Shift — The Triple Dividend of Not Having to Ride
Modal Shift — The Triple Dividend

降低機車死亡最根本的工程，不是要求騎士更小心，而是讓人們有「可以不騎車」的選項。第八節的城鄉光譜已經給了答案：機車死亡佔比最低的台北（47.7%），正是大眾運輸最密集的城市；最高的屏東、高雄（逾 70%），則是公共運輸最稀疏之處。捷運、公車、安全的步行與自行車網絡，就是降低機車死亡的根本基礎建設。The most fundamental engineering for cutting motorcycle deaths is not demanding more care from riders, but giving people the option to "not ride." Section 8's urban-rural spectrum already answered it: Taipei, with the lowest motorcyclist death share (47.7%), is the city with the densest public transport; Pingtung and Kaohsiung, the highest (over 70%), are where public transport is sparsest. Metro, buses, and safe walking and cycling networks are the foundational infrastructure for fewer motorcycle deaths.

運具移轉之所以值得全力投資，是因為它一次帶來三重紅利——這也正是本 atlas「雙齊零願景」與「複利效應」的核心：Modal shift is worth investing in fully because it delivers a triple dividend at once — which is exactly the core of this atlas's "Dual-Zero Vision" and "Compound Effect":

安全紅利Safety dividend

每一趟從機車轉移到捷運／公車的旅次，都是一次幾乎歸零的死亡風險。少騎一公里，就少一公里的暴露。Every trip shifted from scooter to metro/bus is a near-zero death risk. One kilometre less ridden is one kilometre less exposure.

氣候紅利Climate dividend

運輸是台灣碳排與空污的大宗。移轉到大眾運輸與綠運具，直接削減碳排與都市空污。Transport is a major source of Taiwan's carbon and air pollution. Shifting to public and green transport directly cuts carbon and urban air pollution.

健康紅利Health dividend

步行與自行車是日常身體活動；減少機車通勤也降低騎士自身吸入的高濃度廢氣。Walking and cycling are everyday physical activity; less scooter commuting also lowers the high-concentration exhaust riders themselves inhale.

必須強調：運具移轉不是「反機車」，更不是羞辱騎士。它要做的，是把機車從「被迫的唯一選項」，還原成「眾多選項之一」。當人們真的可以不騎車，騎車就會回到它合理的尺度——短程、低速、可被安全道路寬容的尺度。給選擇，而不是給譴責。To be clear: modal shift is not "anti-motorcycle," still less shaming riders. What it does is return the scooter from "the forced only option" to "one option among many." When people truly can choose not to ride, riding returns to its reasonable scale — short, slow, the kind a safe road can forgive. Give choice, not condemnation.

11
課責的方向：誰製造了風險，誰就該共同承擔Where Accountability Should Fall
Where Accountability Should Fall

「溫柔堅定」的核心命題只有一句：把責任從「個別騎士」，移回「形塑系統並從中獲利的環節」。「請大家騎車小心一點」從來不是政策，而是把系統的失敗，偽裝成個人的疏忽。以下是四個可以著力的課責方向——溫柔，是因為我們不究責任何一位逝者；堅定，是因為製造風險、從中得利者，理應共同承擔降低風險的責任。The "gentle but firm" thesis is a single sentence: move responsibility from "the individual rider" back to "those who shape the system and profit from it." "Please everyone ride carefully" was never a policy — it disguises the system's failure as personal negligence. Here are four directions for accountability — gentle, because we blame none of the dead; firm, because those who manufacture risk and gain from it ought to share the duty of reducing it.

Lever · 最大槓桿greatest leverage

政府Government

系統的設計者Designer of the system
  • 運輸投資的城鄉再平衡：把資源投向最缺乏替代運具的縣市Urban-rural rebalancing of transport investment: direct resources to the counties most starved of alternatives
  • 道路與路口的 Vision Zero 設計：機車庇護空間、專用轉向時相、降速Vision Zero road and junction design: motorcycle refuge space, dedicated turn phases, lower speeds
  • 速度管理與分級發照、駕訓制度的改革Speed management, graduated licensing and driver-training reform
  • 土地使用：以混合使用與密度，減少「非移動不可」的距離Land use: mixed use and density to shrink the distances people are forced to travel
Producer · 延伸生產者責任extended producer responsibility

機車製造商Motorcycle makers

風險的生產與行銷者Producers and marketers of risk
  • 安全標配：將 ABS／CBS 等防護科技列為全車系標準，而非高階選配Safety as standard: make ABS/CBS standard across all models, not a premium option
  • 誠實行銷：停止向青年販售「速度與自由」的危險想像Honest marketing: stop selling young people the dangerous fantasy of "speed and freedom"
  • 提撥安全與運具轉型基金，分擔其產品外部成本Fund safety and mode-transition programmes, sharing their products' external costs
  • 電動化與低污染設計，內部化噪音與空污成本Electrification and low-emission design to internalise noise and air-pollution costs
Insurer · 風險的定價者pricer of risk

保險公司Insurers

資料與誘因的掌握者Holders of data and incentives
  • 以費率獎勵安全行為（速度、里程、護具）而非齊頭式定價Reward safe behaviour (speed, mileage, protective gear) in premiums, not flat pricing
  • 確保強制險（強制責任險）對死傷者的保障真正充足Ensure compulsory liability insurance genuinely covers victims adequately
  • 開放去識別化理賠資料，供研究與道路工程改善使用Open de-identified claims data for research and road-engineering improvement

  • 補貼大眾運輸通勤，而非無限供應的免費停車位Subsidise transit commuting instead of endless free parking
  • 支持混合使用與可步行的職住環境，縮短被迫移動Support mixed-use, walkable live-work environments that shorten forced travel
  • 把員工的通勤安全，納入企業的照顧責任Bring employees' commute safety into corporate duty of care
課責 ≠ 究責個人。Accountability ≠ blaming individuals.它不是要找一個壞人來懲罰，而是讓「誰製造了風險、誰從中獲利、誰有能力改變」這三件事被清楚指認，並要求他們共同承擔。當死亡是系統的產出，責任就該指向系統的設計者與獲利者。It is not about finding a villain to punish, but about naming clearly who manufactures the risk, who profits from it, and who has the power to change it — and asking them to share the burden. When death is an output of the system, responsibility should point to the system's designers and beneficiaries.
我們不羞辱任何一位騎士，也不主張一夕禁絕機車。
我們主張的是：把選擇權還給人民，把責任還給系統。We shame no rider, and we do not call for banning the motorcycle overnight.
What we call for is this: give the choice back to the people, and the responsibility back to the system.
Give people the choice to step off the seat — and ask those who built the system, and profit from it, to lead the way down. Gently, but firmly.

Data & Method

資料來源：Source:交通部公路局／道安資訊平台 A1 類（24 小時內死亡）逐案資料，2016–2025，全 22 縣市，共 17,175 名死者，其中機車騎士 10,891 人。Case-level A1 data (death within 24 h) from Taiwan's MOTC Highway Bureau / road-safety information platform, 2016–2025, all 22 counties — 17,175 deaths in total, of which 10,891 were motorcyclists.

「機車死者」認定：Defining a "motorcyclist death":本頁以每案的「最脆弱當事人運具」（mode）為機車者計為機車死亡事故；死者年齡／性別取該案中運具為「機車」之當事人欄位，而非逕取第一當事人（第一當事人多為肇事方／駕駛，性別嚴重偏男，會造成偏誤）。A crash is counted as a motorcyclist death when its "most-vulnerable party mode" is motorcycle; the victim's age/sex is taken from the party whose vehicle is "motorcycle," not from the first party (the first party is usually the at-fault driver and skews heavily male, which would bias the result).

細項欄位涵蓋：Detail-field coverage:天候、光線、道路類型、速限、肇因等詳細欄位僅 2018 年起的「完整格式」案件具備（7,739 件，占 71%）；2016、2017、2021 年部分為簡式紀錄，故肇因／天候／路段等圖表以完整格式案件為母體，趨勢與佔比則使用全部案件。各圖表標題已標明母體。Detailed fields (weather, light, road type, speed limit, cause) exist only for "full-format" cases from 2018 on (7,739 cases, 71%); parts of 2016, 2017 and 2021 are simplified records. So the cause/weather/road-segment charts use the full-format cases as their base, while trend and share use all cases. Each chart caption states its base.

肇因分類：Cause grouping:原始肇因代碼逾百種，本頁依關鍵字歸併為 10 大類；「酒（毒）駕」「逆向」「超速」優先於其他類別判定。The raw cause codes number over a hundred; this page groups them by keyword into 10 categories, with "drink/drug driving," "wrong-way" and "speeding" taking priority over other categories.

外部性數據（§09）：Externality figures (§09):機車保有量 1,466 萬輛為交通部統計查詢網 2025 年初數字；公路運輸約佔 PM2.5 三成、私人汽機車為其中大宗，引自環境部與相關研究；通勤暴露濃度（機車 32.1、汽車 7.6 µg/m³）引自臺北都會區通勤空污暴露調查。需特別說明：機車並非台灣 PM2.5 的單一最大來源，本頁無意將污染責任獨歸騎士——這些數據旨在指出機車的外部性具系統性，正呼應「課責回到系統」的主張。The 14.66-million scooter fleet is the MOTC statistics portal's early-2025 figure; road transport's ~one-third share of PM2.5, with private cars and scooters the bulk, is drawn from the Ministry of Environment and related research; commute-exposure concentrations (scooter 32.1, car 7.6 µg/m³) come from a Taipei-metro commuting air-pollution exposure survey. To be clear: motorcycles are not Taiwan's single largest PM2.5 source, and this page does not assign pollution blame to riders alone — these figures show that motorcycle externalities are systemic, echoing the "accountability back to the system" argument.
